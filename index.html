1. Everyday Clothes

These are just the things you throw on every day – comfy stuff for hanging out, going to work, or whatever.

Tops

T-shirts – You know, cotton or those mixed fabrics; crew necks, V-necks, polos; short or long sleeves. Great by themselves or under something else.

Shirts – Button-down or just regular shirts; cotton, linen, or those man-made fabrics; can look nice or be super casual.

Blouses – These are for women; cotton, silk, or those fake fabrics; short or long sleeves; dressed up or down.

Sweaters &amp; Hoodies – Those soft, fuzzy things or knitted ones; you pull them over your head or zip them up; good for when it’s chilly.

Bottoms

Trousers/Pants – Cotton, linen, wool blends, the fake stuff; dressy like slacks or comfy like chinos or sweats.

Jeans/Denim – Tough and casual; skinny, straight, bootcut, or loose.

Shorts – Cotton, denim, or athletic stuff; for hot days.

Skirts – Cotton, denim, or fake fabrics; short, medium, or long.

Undergarments

Underwear – Cotton, that smooth stuff; briefs, boxers, or panties.

Socks – Cotton, wool, or those fake fabrics; ankle-high, crew, or knee-high.

Undershirts/Camisoles – Light and thin, usually cotton; you wear them under other shirts.

Accessories

Belts, hats, scarves, and simple jewelry. They look good and also do stuff.

2. Seasonal Wear

These are clothes made for certain weather, so you're comfy and don't freeze or burn up.

Summer / Hot Weather

Light Tops – Cotton, linen; no sleeves or short sleeves.

Shorts &amp; Skirts – Fabrics that breathe; loose-fitting.

Dresses – Floaty, light fabrics.

Swimwear – Bathing suits and cover-ups.

Hats &amp; Sunglasses – To keep the sun off you.

Light Shoes – Sandals, flip-flops, easy slip-ons.

Winter / Cold Weather

Sweaters &amp; Cardigans – Wool, that super soft wool, fleece, or fake fabrics.

Coats &amp; Jackets – Down jackets, parkas, wool coats, waterproof jackets.

Thermal Wear – To wear under your clothes to keep you warm.

Scarves, Gloves, Beanies – Wool, fleece, or that fake stuff.

Shoes – Boots (leather, waterproof, insulated).

Rainy / Monsoon

Raincoats / Ponchos – Waterproof, light.

Umbrellas – Gotta have one.

Shoes – Rubber boots, shoes that don't soak up water.

Spring &amp; Fall

Light Jackets &amp; Cardigans – Good for putting on and taking off.

Long-Sleeve Shirts / Tunics – Just right for when it's not too hot or cold.

Closed Shoes / Sneakers – Good for mild weather.

3. Shoes

Shoes for different things, made of different stuff, in different styles.

Casual Shoes

Sneakers / Trainers – Canvas, leather, or that fake stuff; comfy for every day.

Slip-ons / Loafers – Easy to get on and off, casual look.

Sandals / Flip-Flops – Airy and light.

Dressy Shoes

Dress Shoes – Leather, that fake stuff; oxfords, brogues, or heels.

Flats – Closed-toe or open-toe for women; simple for dressy events.

Shoes for Weather

Boots – Leather, waterproof, insulated; for work or winter.

Rain Shoes – Rubber or waterproof stuff.

Hiking / Outdoor Shoes – Tough for walking in the woods.

Athletic Shoes

Running Shoes – Soft and supportive.

Training Shoes – Good for the gym.

Sport Shoes – Football boots, tennis shoes, etc.

4. What Clothes Are Made Of

Cotton – It breathes, it's soft, good for every day and summer.

Linen – Light, it breathes, good for summer.

Wool / Cashmere – Warm, keeps you from freezing in winter.

Synthetic Blends – Polyester, nylon, spandex; tough, stretchy, dries fast.

Denim – Thick cotton; casual and strong.

Leather / Fake Leather – For shoes, jackets, belts, etc.

Fleece / Microfiber – Soft, warm, for jackets and comfy clothes.

5. How to Dress Every Day and for the Weather

Layering – Wear a few things so you can take them off or put them on when the weather changes.

Comfy vs Style – Find the right fabrics, the right fit, so you can move around.

Taking Care of Clothes – Wash them the way the tag says so they last.

Clothes that Work for Everything – Simple colors and styles that you can wear any time of year.

Shoes That Work – Make sure your shoes are right for what you're doing and the weather.
